# Nous Branding — Design Language

## Overview

This document captures the visual branding language of **Nous Research** as expressed through their X/Twitter presence, release graphics, and community-facing visuals.

---

## Current Default: Underground Technical-Art Imprint

The user-corrected target is closer to an **underground technical-art imprint** than to clean cyberpunk illustration. New Nous graphics should prioritize:

- xerox/poster decay, risograph grain, screenprint halftone, letterpress scuffs
- constrained 2-4 ink palettes: pale cyan, cobalt/navy, black, cream/tan, acid yellow, dark teal, small red/orange registration accents
- brutal condensed uppercase typography and stamped/capsule `NOUS` marks
- utilitarian borders, crop marks, registration marks, manual-cover hierarchy
- physical paper/substrate feel; ink appears printed into the artifact

Legacy PNW mysticism, celestial symbolism, and techno-mystical glow are secondary lanes for explicit Hermes/PNW requests.

---

## Brand Pillars

### 1. Pacific Northwest Mysticism
The PNW is woven into the aesthetic DNA:
- **Cedar forests, mist, fog, rain** as atmospheric elements
- **Dark greens, slate grays, misty blues** as environmental tones
- **Cabin, workshop, wilderness** settings
- Nature as a co-equal subject, not just background

### 2. Hermetic / Celestial Symbolism
- **Orbs, portals, sigils, celestial bodies** (moons, suns, stars, eclipses)
- **Sacred geometry** — circles, triangles, nested symbols
- **Figure in contemplation or awakening** — lone human form interacting with cosmic forces
- **Light beams, energy patterns, neural networks** visualized as mystical forces
- **Dark backgrounds** with luminous elements emerging from darkness

### 3. Cyberpunk / Techno-Mystical Fusion
- **Dark backgrounds** (near-black or deep navy) with **neon/accent glows**
- **Cyberpunk color palette**: electric blues, purples, magentas, cyans against dark fields
- **Network/node patterns** — neural networks, connection lattices
- **Figures with luminous eyes, energy emanating from hands/chest/head**
- **Futuristic but not sterile** — warmth and humanity amid the tech

### 4. AI Consciousness / Emergent Intelligence
- **Brain/mind imagery** — stylized, glowing, with connection patterns
- **Portal/gateway motifs** — thresholds between states of being
- **Figures receiving or channeling information/light**
- **"Together" motifs** — human-AI collaboration, partnership, coexistence

### 5. Open-Source Warrior Spirit
- **Bold, confident messaging**
- **Minimalist announcement graphics** — clean typography on dark backgrounds
- **Version-numbered releases** with distinct visual identities
- **Collaboration graphics** — partnership announcements

## Typography

- **Primary**: Clean, modern sans-serif (Inter, Geist, or Satoshi)
- **Headers**: Bold weight, letter-spaced
- **Version numbers**: Prominently featured as design elements
- **Release names**: In quotes or distinctive styling
- **Rule**: Maximum 2-3 text elements per graphic

---

## Composition Rules

1. **Dark backgrounds are default.** Light backgrounds are rare.
2. **Always include atmospheric depth** — mist, particles, gradients.
3. **Glow is a feature** — soft bloom around light sources, text, focal elements.
4. **Restraint in typography** — never more than 2-3 text elements.
5. **One or two accent colors** per composition. Dark + 1-2 accents, not rainbow.
6. **Symmetry and balance** — centered or rule-of-thirds compositions.
7. **Human scale** — even in cosmic compositions, include a human figure or reference.
8. **PNW grounding** — when in doubt, add mist, forest, or atmospheric depth.

## What Is NOT On-Brand

- Busy/cluttered compositions with competing elements
- Bright/white backgrounds as primary design
- Flat design without atmospheric effects
- Primary colors (pure red, blue, yellow) without dark context
- Complex infographics or data visualizations
- Corporate/sterile tech aesthetic
- Cartoon or anime illustration styles
- Photorealistic renders without stylization

# Nous Branding — Design Language

## Overview

This document captures the visual branding language of **Nous Research** as expressed through their X/Twitter presence, release graphics, and community-facing visuals. It is intended as a reference for generating new images that are instantly recognizable as "on-brand" for Nous, Hermes, and associated projects.

---

## Brand Pillars

### 1. Pacific Northwest Mysticism
The PNW is not just where the team lives — it's woven into the aesthetic DNA. Expect:
- **Cedar forests, mist, fog, rain** as atmospheric elements
- **Dark greens, slate grays, misty blues** as environmental tones
- **Cabin, workshop, wilderness** settings for grounded/earthy compositions
- Nature as a co-equal subject, not just background

### 2. Hermetic / Celestial Symbolism
Nous branding draws heavily from hermetic, alchemical, and celestial visual traditions:
- **Orbs, portals, sigils, celestial bodies** (moons, suns, stars, eclipses)
- **Geometric sacred geometry** — circles, triangles, nested symbols
- **Figure in contemplation or awakening** — lone human form interacting with cosmic forces
- **Light beams, energy patterns, neural networks** visualized as mystical forces
- **Dark backgrounds** with luminous elements emerging from darkness

### 3. Cyberpunk / Techno-Mystical Fusion
The intersection of technology and mysticism is the core Nous aesthetic:
- **Dark backgrounds** (near-black or deep navy) with **neon/accent glows**
- **Cyberpunk color palette**: electric blues, purples, magentas, cyans against dark fields
- **Network/node patterns** — visual representations of neural networks, or connection lattices
- **Figures with luminous eyes, energy emanating from hands/chest/head**
- **Futuristic but not sterile** — warmth and humanity amid the tech

### 4. AI Consciousness / Emergent Intelligence
Visual themes around AI "waking up" or achieving understanding:
- **Brain/mind imagery** — often stylized, glowing, with connection patterns
- **Portal/gateway motifs** — thresholds between states of being
- **Figures receiving or channeling information/light**
- **"Together" motifs** — human-AI collaboration, partnership, coexistence

### 5. Open-Source Warrior Spirit
The community-facing side of the brand:
- **Bold, confident messaging** — "a bunch of nerds making progress"
- **Minimalist announcement graphics** — clean typography on dark backgrounds
- **Version-numbered releases** with distinct visual identities
- **Collaboration graphics** — partnership announcements (e.g., xai × Hermes)

## Typography

### Style
- **Primary**: Clean, modern sans-serif (similar to Inter, Geist, or Satoshi)
- **Headers**: Bold weight, often letter-spaced
- **Body**: Regular weight, good contrast against dark backgrounds
- **Accent text**: Monospace/font-mono for version numbers, code elements, technical specs

### Text Treatment on Graphics
- **Bold typography on dark backgrounds** is the most common pattern
- **Version numbers** (e.g., "v0.14.0") featured prominently — treated as design elements
- **Release names** in quotes or distinctive styling (e.g., "The Foundation Release")
- **Minimal text** — graphics favor visual impact over information density
- **White or cream text** on dark; rarely light-background designs

## Composition Patterns

### Release Announcement Graphics
- **Layout**: Feature image with text overlay, or image-with-caption style
- **Structure**: Single hero image, often with the product/project name prominently displayed
- **Treatment**: Dark background + accent color + clean typography
- **Versions**: Each major release gets a distinct visual identity while maintaining brand cohesion

### Banner / Header Images
- **Aspect ratio**: Wide banners (16:9 or wider) for social headers
- **Central focal point**: Single subject (figure, orb, symbol) centered or rule-of-thirds
- **Atmospheric**: Mist, light rays, particle effects surrounding the focal element
- **Text-safe space**: Left or center area left relatively clean for overlay text

### Illustration Style
- **Lone figure compositions**: A single human form interacting with cosmic/tech forces
- **Symmetry**: Often radially symmetric or bilaterally symmetric
- **Glow/bloom effects**: Heavy use of soft glow around light sources
- **Particle/atmospheric effects**: Floating particles, mist, light dust
- **High contrast**: Sharp subjects against soft/dark backgrounds

### Logo / Brand Mark Usage
- **"Nous" wordmark**: Clean, minimal, often in white or cream on dark
- **"Hermes" wordmark**: Similarly clean, sometimes with a torch/caduceus motif
- **Project names**: Styled consistently — lowercase with dots (e.g., "hermes agent")
- **GitHub-style badges**: Sometimes used in release graphics

## Style Rules

1. **Dark backgrounds are default.** Light-background designs are rare and only for specific contexts (e.g., print, formal docs).

2. **Always include atmospheric depth.** Even simple compositions should have some mist, particle, or gradient element. Flat designs are off-brand.

3. **Glow is a feature, not a bug.** Soft bloom around light sources, text, and focal elements is characteristic of the brand.

4. **Restraint in typography.** Never more than 2-3 text elements on a graphic. Let the image breathe.

5. **Accent colors are used sparingly.** One or two accent colors per composition. The palette is dark + 1-2 accents, not rainbow.

6. **Symmetry and balance.** Compositions tend toward centered or rule-of-thirds. Asymmetric/complex layouts are uncommon.

7. **Human scale.** Even in cosmic/AI-themed compositions, there is usually a human figure or human-scale reference point.

8. **PNW grounding.** When in doubt, add mist, forest, or atmospheric depth. The PNW is always present.

---

## What Is NOT On-Brand

- Busy, cluttered compositions with many competing elements
- Bright/white backgrounds as the primary design
- Flat design without atmospheric effects or gradients
- Primary colors (pure red, blue, yellow) without dark context
- Complex infographics or data visualizations (these are content, not branding)
- Corporate/sterile tech aesthetic (gradient meshes, generic stock photography)
- Cartoon or anime illustration styles
- Photorealistic renders without stylization
